# Opponent — Round 1

## Position
Hierarchical-actor (options / FeUdal-style) is the higher-leverage next investment than BC-bootstrap, because it directly attacks the *root cause* of the 40h stall — a 20-to-100× mismatch between the actor's imagination horizon and the game's natural macro-action timescales — while BC-bootstrap papers over symptoms, is capped by demonstrator skill, requires 40-100h of expensive skilled labor, and is fundamentally incompatible with our current stripped action space and obs schema.

## Steelman of the pro-BC side (concede first, then attack)
Yes: the 40h Dreamer run is provably learning nothing (`actor_entropy=3.804 / 3.807` — 99.92% uniform after 346 k env-steps, agent3-adversarial-audit §Executive Summary). Yes: NetHack, StarCraft II, and Dota all used demo bootstraps and *pure* cold-start RL is compute-infeasible on a 3060 Ti at 2.36 SPS with n_envs=2. Yes: a BC actor initialised from any coherent human trajectory will, in expectation, beat a uniform-random policy on `rooms_visited` and `boss_kills` on day one. Nobody is arguing that BC has *zero* value. The argument is that (a) it does not fix the load-bearing failure mode, (b) its expected gain is far smaller than the audit assumes once you price the demo-collection labor, obs-mismatch, and action-space amputation, and (c) hierarchical-actor produces a strictly larger horizon-collapse effect for comparable or lower engineering cost.

## Objections

### 1. Temporal horizon is the root cause of the stall, and BC does not touch it. *Load-bearing. Confidence: High.*
- **Failure mode.** The audit itself quantifies the mismatch: `imag_horizon = 20 ticks × (1/15 Hz) = 1.33 s`; `seq_len = 32 ticks = 2.13 s`. Isaac macro-action timescales: room clear 5–20 s (4–15× the window), boss fight 30–60 s (22–45×), full-floor traversal 60–120 s (**45–90×**) (agent3-adversarial-audit §Prop D). With `cont ≈ 1` almost everywhere and `gamma=0.999`, `loss/actor_target_mean = 47` with `loss/actor_adv_abs_mean = 0.037` — a **1270:1 target-to-advantage ratio** — meaning every action looks equally-valued after EMA whitening. This is a value-inflation loop caused by horizon truncation, not by a bad prior on the actor's weights.
- **Why BC does not fix it.** Initialising the actor from BC weights changes the *policy prior*. It does not change `imag_horizon`, it does not change the `[-20, +20]` symlog-disc value clipping that saturates below `r_beat_mom = +50`, and it does not change the fact that the critic bootstraps at step 20 with a hallucinated `V ≈ 47` regardless of the actor. Within 5–10 k gradient steps the actor's BC prior will be washed out by the same runaway-target signal that already flattened the current policy to uniform. **A BC prior is a starting condition; the horizon problem is a fixed-point attractor.** Historical parallel: the "no-op action" issue in Atari DQN (Mnih 2015) was not solved by imitation priors — it required either frame-skip changes or n-step returns, i.e. horizon-side interventions.
- **Why hierarchical fixes it directly.** Options collapse the credit-assignment horizon from ~1800 primitive ticks per episode to ~10–20 option-invocations — the audit's own estimate is a **200× reduction** (agent3-adversarial-audit §Top-3 Fix #3). At option resolution, `imag_horizon = 20 options ≈ 100 s of game time`, which covers full room clears and most boss fights. The value target stops running away because option-termination predicates ground the return computation in real game state changes (room cleared, coin count decremented, active-charge reset). This is a known-good fix pattern: FeUdal Networks (Vezhnevets et al. 2017, [arXiv:1703.01161](https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.01161)) show 3–5× sample-efficiency gains on Montezuma's Revenge — a game with essentially identical temporal structure (long-horizon, sparse-reward, room-based). Option-Critic (Bacon, Harb, Precup 2017, [arXiv:1609.05140](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.05140)) provides end-to-end differentiable option discovery on the exact primitive action space we have. HAAR (Li et al. NeurIPS 2019, [proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2019/hash/81e74d678581a3bb7a720b019f4f1a93](https://papers.nips.cc/paper_files/paper/2019/file/81e74d678581a3bb7a720b019f4f1a93-Paper.pdf)) demonstrates HRL specifically for "long-horizon problems with sparse and delayed rewards" — the exact regime we are in.
- **Worst-case if we do BC instead.** Best case: 10–20 % improvement on rooms_visited during the BC-only phase, followed by a fine-tune stage that hits the same horizon wall the current run hit — probability that fine-tune collapses back to entropy≈max within 20 k gradient steps: >50 % on my reading, because none of the diagnosed pathologies (value clipping, cont≈1, RND dead) have been repaired.

### 2. BC-bootstrap has a labor-cost floor that the audit under-prices by ~1 OOM. *Load-bearing. Confidence: High.*
- **Failure mode.** The audit's Fix #1 asks for "2–3 humans, 20–50h of Basement I → Womb runs" (agent3-adversarial-audit §Top-3 Fix #1). This vastly under-prices what "usable BC data" actually costs.
  1. **Skilled** Isaac players are required, not warm bodies. Northernlion clears Mom in Basement I → Womb runs at ~90 %; a median player is well under 50 %. BC of a median player teaches median behaviour, which caps our ceiling below "get past Basement II."
  2. Demos must be **recorded through our mod** to produce `(obs, action)` pairs in our exact schema. YouTube VODs are unusable directly (no obs; humans see pixels). This means humans play *inside our RL bridge* — at 15 Hz control with our action space, which is *not the game they know how to play*. Realistic effective demo throughput: 0.3–0.5× real-time due to unfamiliar control.
  3. Repentance runs are 20–45 min each; a "40 h dataset" is 60–120 runs. If the seed distribution has to cover Basement I through Depths II across characters, effective coverage per hour is thin.
  4. At even a symbolic $20/h × 50 h × 3 humans, that is **$3 k in labour** or ~150 h of founder time, before any Iteration 2 re-collection.
- **Re-collection cost is near-certain.** The obs schema is going to change: the audit's Fix #2 (add active-charge, transformations, minimap, door-target-types, cards/pills/trinket) is called out as a prerequisite — "do #2 *before* #1 records" (agent3-adversarial-audit §Top-3 Fix #2). Any BC dataset recorded on the current obs schema is **stranded** the moment we ship the obs expansion the audit demands. Historical base rate for schema-stability in an RL research codebase over 6 months: <30 % (my prior; the isaac_rl `reward.py` alone has v0 → v1 → v1.5 → v2 → v3 in ~2 months per agent3-adversarial-audit §Prop C).
- **Worst-case impact.** Two demo-collection cycles × $3 k × 3 humans = $18 k or ~450 founder-hours, before RL fine-tune even starts. Hierarchical-actor engineering estimate: ~1–2 weeks of one engineer defining 6–10 options + termination predicates in Python (no external labour, no dataset). Cost ratio: **~10-30× in favour of hierarchical.**

### 3. Human obs ≠ our obs. BC on partial obs learns the wrong policy. *Load-bearing. Confidence: High.*
- **Failure mode.** The audit's own Prop B: "a *human* watching only the numeric state vector cannot decide whether to take a Devil deal — the deal's terms (item quality, HP cost) aren't observable... If the human can't play, the agent can't either." Missing from current obs: **minimap topology, active-item charge and ID, transformation counters, card/pill/trinket slots, door target-room types beyond {BOSS,TREASURE,SECRET}, item pool state, boss-room-found flag** (agent3-adversarial-audit §Prop B). A human demonstrator conditions on 15+ HUD features the agent literally does not receive.
- **Consequence for BC.** BC is `p(a | obs)`. If the human's true policy is `p_H(a | obs, minimap, active_charge, transformation_counters, HUD)`, then BC on our obs converges to `E_{missing | obs}[p_H(a | obs, missing)]` — a *marginalised* policy that averages over information the agent then has no way to condition on at inference. This is textbook covariate shift (Ross & Bagnell 2011, DAgger, [arXiv:1011.0686](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~sross1/publications/Ross-AIStats11-NoRegret.pdf)) compounded with an information gap. Expected symptom: BC actor picks up any pedestal indiscriminately (because human "walk to pedestal" action doesn't depend on quality within the obs), spends actives at random floors (because human "press space" depends on active_charge which we don't ship), and takes Devil deals uniformly (because human "walk through devil door" depends on the item behind it which we don't preview).
- **Base-rate violation.** Ren et al. 2021 ([Generalization Guarantees for Imitation Learning](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v155/ren21a/ren21a.pdf)) show BC generalisation error scales with `sqrt(H · d_H(π_learned, π_expert))` where `H` is the horizon and `d_H` is a Hellinger distance that *grows with the size of the unobserved state components*. For a game where the human's Q-function depends on unobserved minimap + HUD, `d_H` is Ω(1), not `o(1)`. Compounding-error bound is quadratic in `H = 1800`: `T² = 3.24 × 10^6` cumulative-mistake regret.
- **The "just fix obs first" counter fails.** If we have to expand the obs to make BC viable (audit's own recommendation), then we have paid the obs-expansion cost anyway — at which point hierarchical-actor is *strictly cheaper marginal* than hierarchical + obs-expansion + demo-collection + BC + fine-tune. The Bayesian dominance is one-sided.

### 4. Stripped action space MultiDiscrete([9, 5]) cannot represent the demonstrator's actions. *Load-bearing. Confidence: High.*
- **Failure mode.** The current action head is move-9 × shoot-5 only. The `use_active / drop_bomb / pill_card` heads were REMOVED on 2026-07-02 (agent2-character-strategy §Reward function character-awareness). Skilled Isaac play uses space bar constantly:
  - **D6** — Isaac's *starting active* — reroll pedestal (Q4 fisher). Northernlion uses it 3–8×/floor.
  - **Book of Belial** (Judas) — mandatory pre-boss damage buff.
  - **Book of Virtues** (Bethany) — every fight.
  - **Pandora's Box, Mama Mega, Crooked Penny, Glowing Hour Glass** — run-defining single uses.
  - **Bombs** — mandatory for secret rooms, some boss fights, tinted rocks.
  - **Cards / pills** — Soul of Isaac, Chaos Card, Get Out of Jail, Rune of Dagaz.
- **Consequence for BC.** Every demo frame where the human pressed space, dropped a bomb, or used a card must be either (a) discarded — throwing out 5–20 % of the dataset, biased toward the highest-information moments (boss-fight setups, pedestal-choice moments); or (b) mapped to no-op — actively teaching the agent to ignore the same moments. Either way, BC on stripped action space is *guaranteed* to teach a policy that never uses D6, never bombs a secret room, never uses a card. That's **exactly the failure mode already visible in the 40 h run** (`use_item=0, bombs_used_max=1, keys_used=0` per agent3-adversarial-audit §Prop E), which BC therefore *cannot* fix by construction.
- **Character kills.** Lilith has 0 base damage; her Incubus fires via space-bar Box of Friends. **Lilith is literally unplayable with the current action space** (agent2-character-strategy §Reward function character-awareness). No BC dataset that includes Lilith gameplay survives action-space projection.
- **Worst case.** BC actor initialised on Isaac-only demos with active-frames dropped produces a policy strictly worse than random on any run where the D6 was needed to break a bad early-floor pool. Probability that BC-fine-tune reaches Mom (Basement I → Depths II → Womb I) without ever using D6 or a bomb: <5 % (empirical: even human speedrunners can't).
- **Restoring the action head is a prerequisite** to BC (audit's Top-3 Fix #3 acknowledges this: "restore active-item action head"). Once again, the *prerequisite work stack* for BC is: fix obs + fix action space + collect demos + train BC + fine-tune. Hierarchical only needs option definitions and termination predicates.

### 5. Procedural generation → BC state coverage is Cantor-thin. *Load-bearing. Confidence: Medium-High.*
- **Failure mode.** Isaac's state manifold is enormous: 700+ collectibles with sparse pickup distributions, 30+ item pools, 2^256+ passive-item combinations exposed to the policy (agent1-item-economy §Gap 4), 15+ transformations, 17 characters × Tainted variants, procedurally-generated floor layouts with hundreds of enemy types × per-room compositions. A 40–100 h demo dataset covers ~120–300 runs — statistically a rounding error against the state manifold.
- **Empirical anchor.** Hambro et al. 2022, "Dungeons and Data" (NetHack, [openreview.net/forum?id=zHNNSzo10xN](https://openreview.net/forum?id=zHNNSzo10xN)) collected **10 billion transitions from humans + 3 billion from AutoAscend bot** and BC still only reached "within top 15 %" of expert mean score — never matched. NetHack has *less* procedural complexity than Repentance per-tile (single sprite tile map vs Isaac's ~40 concurrent projectile/enemy entities + physics). Extrapolating: our realistic 40–100 h ≈ 3–10 million transitions is **3-4 orders of magnitude below** the Dungeons&Data threshold that produced merely-competitive-not-expert BC. Ren et al. 2021 shows offline learning (BC included) has a "generalisation gap" specifically in procedural environments where the test distribution differs from train ([arXiv:2312.05742](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05742)).
- **Consequence.** BC policy transfers only on floor/item combinations that appeared in the demo set; on new procedural instantiations (which is 99 % of runs post-collection), the actor falls back to interpolated behaviour that is not much better than the initial WM-reconstruction-guided prior. Meanwhile hierarchical options like "clear current room" have *no procedural-generation sensitivity* — the option is state-invariant, it terminates on `room_clear=True` regardless of what enemies are in the room.
- **Worst case.** BC dataset "works" on the seed distribution used to record, degrades sharply off-distribution (60–90 % performance drop is typical for offline RL in procgen benchmarks — Cobbe et al. 2020, ProcGen). We would end up training a policy that overfits to Northernlion's specific playstyle on a specific seed range.

### 6. BC ceiling is bounded by demonstrator skill, and our real target (Mother / Delirium) is beyond it. *Load-bearing on multi-stage goals. Confidence: High.*
- **Failure mode.** BC's expected performance is at best `E[reward | π_expert]`. For our current bar ("clear Basement I") this is not a binding constraint — but the entire *point* of this project (agent3-adversarial-audit §Executive summary — "let alone Mom") is to reach later floors. Even top streamers clear Mother at 30–50 %; Delirium <20 %. If we recruit "reasonable Isaac players" (median community skill) as the audit implies, their Basement I clear rate is ~80 % and their Mom clear rate is ~40 % — meaning BC-cloned behaviour on the second half of runs is *learning from failed trajectories*.
- **AlphaStar precedent works AGAINST BC-only.** AlphaStar hit the SL-BC ceiling and required fictitious self-play + prioritised league training to break past it (Vinyals et al. 2019, Nature). We do not have compute for a league. If we hit the BC ceiling and the RL fine-tune horizon problem is unresolved (which is the current state), we are stuck.
- **Hierarchical does not have this ceiling.** Options + intrinsic option-completion rewards + a high-level policy trained by RL can, in principle, exceed human performance on within-option skills (e.g., pixel-perfect boss dodging) and compose them in novel ways.
- **Worst case.** We spend 6 weeks on BC-bootstrap, reach ~median-human performance on Basement I and Caves I, and then the horizon problem re-emerges verbatim on Depths → Womb, where imag_horizon vs floor-length mismatch is *identical* to today. Net progress: one dungeon level. Cost: 6 weeks + $3–18 k demo labour.

### 7. Hierarchical directly unlocks the reward-shaping gaps Agent 1 and Agent 2 identified. *Novel synergy. Confidence: Medium.*
- Agent 1 documents that current reward is uniform (+2.0 per pickup) where the game is 50× non-uniform, and proposes 15 reward changes. Agent 2 documents that reward assumes Isaac semantics and breaks for 11 other characters.
- **These gaps do not disappear under BC** — a BC policy trained on shaped-reward demos inherits the shaping bugs, and there is no gradient from "the reward was mis-specified" to "the human's implicit strategy" that BC can extract.
- **Hierarchical dramatically simplifies reward.** With options like `use_active_item` and `purchase_pedestal`, the terminal reward per option can be *directly* the quality-weighted outcome (Q4 pickup = +6, Plan C use = −20) without needing per-tick shaping. The high-level actor's reward is close to the true objective (floor cleared, boss killed) because option-termination boundaries align with natural game events. This is the "reduce reward engineering by choosing better action abstractions" pattern — Sutton, Precup, Singh 1999, "Between MDPs and semi-MDPs."
- **BC does the opposite** — it locks in the current shaped reward as an implicit prior via the demonstrator's behaviour distribution, making future reward-refactors *harder*.

## Load-bearing vs weak summary

**Load-bearing (kills the thesis if true):**
1. Horizon mismatch is root cause; BC does not fix; options do. [High]
2. BC labor + re-collection cost is ~10-30× hierarchical implementation cost. [High]
3. Obs mismatch turns BC into marginalised-policy noise. [High]
4. Stripped action space makes BC on space-bar/bomb/card actions impossible. [High]
5. Procedural generation → BC coverage is Cantor-thin; NetHack precedent shows 3-4 OOM data gap. [Medium-High]

**Weak / supporting:**
6. BC ceiling caps late-floor performance. [High but only binds after Basement clears.]
7. Hierarchical simplifies reward. [Medium; nice-to-have not kill-shot.]

## Predicted proponent lines and pre-emptive counters

- *"BC is cheap — 4h to train on 3060 Ti"* → training cost is negligible; **collection + re-collection cost** is the killer.
- *"AlphaStar / NetHack proved BC works"* → both used O(10^9)–O(10^10) transitions and still required hierarchy / league. Our budget is O(10^7).
- *"Hierarchical is engineering-heavy — options must be hand-designed"* → 6–10 options with hand-coded termination predicates in Python is 1–2 engineering weeks; option-critic can learn intra-option policies. The audit itself estimates the hierarchical fix at comparable complexity to BC-plus-obs-plus-action-space.
- *"BC and hierarchical are complementary, not exclusive"* → yes, but resource-constrained; the *higher-leverage next investment* is the one that fixes root cause. Hierarchical dominates in leverage-per-engineer-week.
